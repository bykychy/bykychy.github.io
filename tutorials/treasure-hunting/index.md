# ⛏️ Treasure Hunting Technologies

Master the science behind subsurface and near-surface sensing methods used in archaeology, survey, and field exploration. The goal is not mythology or magic detection; it is disciplined reasoning from physical measurements.

<div class="info-box tip">
  <strong>🏔️ Central Asian Context</strong>
  Central Asia — particularly Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan — sits at the crossroads of ancient civilizations. The Silk Road, Scythian burial mounds (kurgans), and Turkic stone monuments make this region incredibly rich for archaeological prospection using modern technology.
</div>

<div class="section-card featured-card">
  <h2>How this section connects to SAR</h2>
  <p>Treasure-hunting and archaeological prospection work best when methods are combined. SAR helps map surface patterns and seasonal wetness, while electromagnetic tools, GPR, and X-ray techniques help narrow down material and depth hypotheses on the ground.</p>
</div>

## Design principle

Every major tutorial in this section is intended to include:

- A physical model of the measurement.
- A mathematical section with units and worked formulas.
- Interpretation examples tied to Central Asia.
- Exercise sets with easy, medium, and hard problems.
